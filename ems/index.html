The Problem

Three problems every energy project faces.

Not edge cases. These are the reasons EMS deployments go over budget, fail utility inspection, or get called back on site.

⚡
Unverified breaker close operations
Most EMS platforms issue a close command and assume execution. No sync-check confirmation, no 52a/52b feedback loop, no phase-angle verification. When a close fails or misoperates, the root cause is buried in relay event logs — discovered after the fact.
"We had an auto-reclose event that took the feeder down. The EMS had no record of it."
📋
No audit trail for interconnection
Utilities and interconnection engineers require complete event logs: every trip, every close, every mode transition — with source attribution and timestamps. Project-specific scripts on a laptop don't satisfy SDG&E, SCE, or CPUC Rule 21 documentation requirements.
"The interconnection engineer asked for 6 months of operational logs. We had a partial CSV."
🔁
Every project re-engineered from scratch
Custom EMS logic written for one site doesn't transfer to the next. Different hardware, different DER mix, different utility — and the engineering cycle restarts. There's no product standard, no template, no repeatable delivery structure.
"We quoted 3 weeks for commissioning. It took 11 weeks and two site visits."

CONTROL PHILOSOPHY

Protection Trip = Relay Primary
The protection relay holds protection engineering responsibility. EnergizeOS™ observes, logs, and reacts — but does not replace the relay. Trip thresholds, settings, and protection coordination remain with the protection engineer.
Close = EMS Only
Breaker close is EMS-exclusive. No blind close. No auto-reclose by default. Every close requires all C1–C7 conditions satisfied, physical 52a/52b feedback confirmed, and admin authorization verified.
Operational Trip = EMS Optional
Controlled disconnection for commissioning or maintenance may be issued by EMS when site design permits. This is a control action — not a protection function — and is clearly distinguished in all event logs.
Responsibility separation is not configurable. Protection responsibility stays with protection hardware and licensed protection engineers. Control and supervisory responsibility belongs to the EMS. This boundary is enforced in hardware and software at every deployment.

C1–C7 Interlock Chain

No blind closes. Every time.

All grid-connection close operations require seven conditions to be satisfied simultaneously. This is enforced in hardware and software — not a configuration option.

C1
Grid Stable ≥ 60 s
Voltage 210–260 V; Frequency 59.5–60.5 Hz sustained ≥ 60 s
← CM-UFD relay / utility-grade meter
C2
Zero Export Verified
Active power < ±50 W; no reverse injection to utility PCC
← Revenue-grade meter (Modbus TCP)
C3
BESS Idle or Charging
PCS state = Idle or Charging only; no active discharge command
← PCS controller status register
C4
Close Authorized
Administrator confirmed via UI; role and session verified
← Local or Remote UI (role check)
C5
Phase Sync Completed
Phase angle difference ≤ 5°; sync relay output confirmed
← Phase synchronizer / sync-check relay
C6
Breaker Currently Open
52b auxiliary contact confirmed OPEN; prevents duplicate close
← Breaker 52b DI hardwired
C7
Admin Role Verified
Active session = Administrator; operator-level close rejected
← UI role and permission module
Conditions C4, C5, C6, C7 not satisfied. Close command blocked.
Protection stays with the relay.
EnergizeOS™ does not replace or overlap with the protection relay. The relay holds protection engineering responsibility. The EMS holds close authorization. These are separate, non-overlapping responsibilities enforced at every level of the system.
Triple-redundant trip architecture.
Every PCC breaker has three independent trip paths: EMS DO signal (software), UVT undervoltage trip (hardware fail-safe activated on UPS loss), and manual field button (hardwired, network-independent). All three operate independently and do not conflict.
Full audit trail — utility-ready.
Every close, trip, mode change, and blocking condition is logged with timestamp, source attribution, and outcome. Exportable in structured format. Accepted by SDG&E and Black & Veatch for Rule 21 interconnection documentation.
Fully offline-capable.
All strategy execution runs on the embedded Local Runtime Engine. Cloud connectivity is optional and used only for OTA updates and remote O&M. If the network drops, the plant continues operating with full interlock enforcement.
