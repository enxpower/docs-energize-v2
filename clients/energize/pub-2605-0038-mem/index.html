On DO2 and RL-02

These are EnergizeOS EMS Control Panel components — not SDG&E documents.

  • DO2 = Digital Output #2 on the EMS I/O module (Moxa ioLogik E1212, terminal TB402-2). This is the output that commands the breaker to close.
  • RL-02 = Close Interposing Relay inside the EMS panel. DO2 energizes RL-02's 24VDC coil. RL-02's NO contact then routes via cross-cabinet wiring to the CR-UC coil in SWBD#1, which drives the 52-U Close Coil (120VAC).

This is the EnergizeOS EMS architecture defined in PUB-2605-0004-SPEC v1.6, shared with B&V in full.

For context: the same two-stage isolation principle (DO → interposing relay → coil) was recommended by Vatsal in his July 25, 2025 email: "Use an intermediate relay between SEL-700G DO and shunt trip coil." We applied the same practice to the close path.

On Items 1b and 1c — A Technical Clarification, Not a Debate

I want to be straightforward here rather than let this become a prolonged discussion.

The core issue is not whether Vatsal approved a particular configuration. The core issue is that MC-01 and U1-01 describe OUT302 as performing opposite functions — one drawing shows it driving the close circuit, the other shows it driving the trip coil. This is a drawing discrepancy, and it means no one currently knows what OUT302 is physically wired to do in the manufactured SWBD#1.

In this context:

  • Item 1b (disconnect OUT302 from CR-UC) is not a scope change request from Energize. It is a necessary resolution to a drawing discrepancy that affects whether the close path is controlled or autonomous. Under California Rule 21 and IEEE 1547-2018, a protective relay output must not be able to close the intertie breaker without EMS authorization. If OUT302 is physically connected to the close circuit, that condition is not met — regardless of what the .RDB says, because .RDB can be changed or overridden in the field.
  • Item 1c (EMS-only close path) was confirmed as the project's control architecture in writing. Vatsal confirmed on July 25, 2025: "EMS will be permitted to issue the close command after verifying that the BESS is back in charge-only mode." This is also documented in PUB-2605-0004-SPEC v1.6 §3. Energize has no interest in scope expansion — we simply need the drawings to reflect the architecture that has already been agreed.

We welcome your protection engineer's review of the auto-reclosure question. To assist that review, the specific risk we identified is this: if OUT302 remains physically connected to CR-UC, any output pulse from OUT302 — whether during normal operation, testing, or a firmware condition — would close the breaker without EMS C1–C7 conditions being satisfied. This is the uncontrolled close risk that Rule 21 is designed to prevent, independent of whether the relay is configured for auto-reclose or not.
