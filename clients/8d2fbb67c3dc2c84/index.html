TECHNICAL POSITION MEMORANDUM

CA-PEP01 · PepsiCo / SDG&E BESS Microgrid EMS · Energize Solutions Inc.

Prepared by: Energize Solutions Inc. · Date: July 16, 2026 · Doc Ref: PUB-2607-0002-MEM

PURPOSE OF THIS MEMORANDUM

This memorandum sets out Energize Solutions' conditional acceptance of the control architecture proposed by B&V's formal ECO response of July 7, 2026, for the CA-PEP01 SWBD#1 SEL-700G / EMS interface. This acceptance is limited to the conceptual division of control responsibility. It does not constitute approval of final RavenVolt drawings, final SEL settings/SELogic equations, field wiring, final RDB implementation, FAT results, or EOR/utility acceptance — those remain subject to design release, testing, and the responsible EOR's approval. The remaining open items are the confirmations requested in Section 3 and the IP address assignments in Section 4.

BACKGROUND

Since the original ECO request dated May 4, 2026 (PUB-2605-0011-PROP), five technical items on SWBD#1 have been under review between Energize, B&V, and Ravenvolt. Section 1 below records the key dates and positions exchanged on these items, for reference and to avoid any ambiguity about what has already been agreed.

Energize accepts B&V's proposed architecture as final for Items 2 and 3 at the conceptual level, subject to the confirmations in Section 3. Detailed implementation — including the final SELogic equations, communication watchdog behavior, and FAT verification — remains open, as set out below.

SECTION 2 — POSITION BY ITEM

Item 1 — SEL-700G Protection Functions (ANSI 27/59/81/32/25)

Status: ARCHITECTURE CONFIRMED — B&V APPROVAL PENDING

Position: B&V EOR confirmed (Jul 7) that ANSI 27/59/81 are required. Energize's architectural position on required protection functions is closed. Final setpoint selection, coordination, field-loading authorization, calibration, trip verification, and SDG&E submission remain open B&V/EOR responsibilities.

Remaining action: B&V/EOR to confirm whether ANSI 81R (ROCOF) is required and, if so, provide the approved setpoint and implementation basis; complete final settings package and submit to SDG&E.

Item 2 — OUT301/OUT302 — CR-UC Close Path Architecture

Status: CONCEPTUALLY ACCEPTED — FINAL LOGIC PENDING

Position: B&V's proposed architecture is accepted at the conceptual level: the EMS provides the system-level operational close permissive; the SEL-700G retains final protective supervision and the physical close output. Absence or loss of the EMS permissive shall block all automatic and remote close commands.

Remaining action: B&V/Ravenvolt to confirm the communication-health logic in Section 3, and to provide the final SELogic (CLX) equation for review prior to FAT.

Item 3 — 52a/52b Auxiliary Contacts — Signal Path Architecture

Status: CONCEPTUALLY ACCEPTED — VALIDATION PENDING

Position: B&V's single-path architecture — 52a/52b wired only to SEL-700G, with EMS reading breaker status via Modbus TCP — is accepted at the conceptual level, subject to the stale-data handling described in Section 3.

Remaining action: B&V/Ravenvolt to confirm EMS fail-safe behavior on communication loss (Section 3). Communication-loss and stale-data conditions to be verified during FAT.

Item 4 — SDG&E Final Approval Submission

Status: B&V/EOR LEAD — FINAL ACCEPTANCE PENDING

Position: SDG&E has conditionally pre-approved the non-export relay scheme. B&V/EOR retains overall responsibility for the final protection package, setting basis, coordination, certification, and SDG&E submission.

Remaining action: Energize will provide EMS-specific configuration records and test evidence as expressly identified in the approved scope or under a separately authorized support arrangement.

Item 5 — Static IP Address Allocation

Status: ENERGIZE INTERNAL ADDRESSES DEFINED

Position: Energize's internal EMS panel addressing uses subnet 192.168.111.0/24. Full table provided in Section 4.

Remaining action: B&V/GC to confirm whether SEL-700G, SEL-735, and the management switch will join this subnet or be assigned under the site OT addressing/VLAN plan, and update drawing COM-01 accordingly.

SECTION 3 — Required Confirmations

1. Communication-loss behavior (Item 2): Please confirm that loss, timeout, invalidation, or restart of the EMS–SEL communication path will force the SEL close equation to a non-permissive state, irrespective of the last transmitted RBxx value. Please identify the communication-health operand, timeout value, relay/EMS restart behavior, and reauthorization sequence used in the final SELogic equation.

2. Stale-data handling (Item 3): When breaker-status data becomes stale or unavailable, the EMS shall inhibit all close-command issuance and identify breaker status as invalid/unknown. The HMI may retain the last-known value for diagnostic display only, clearly marked as stale, but this value shall not be used as a valid input to any close permissive logic. Please confirm this behavior is implemented.

Written confirmation of these two points is required to close the design-review stage of Items 2 and 3. Final closure of both items also requires successful FAT verification of communication-loss and stale-data conditions — see Section 5.

SECTION 5 — FAT Verification

The written confirmations in Section 3 close the design-review stage of Items 2 and 3. Full closure additionally requires FAT demonstration that: (a) the close permissive is denied when the EMS–SEL communication path is lost, timed out, or invalid; and (b) the EMS correctly inhibits close commands and flags breaker status as invalid when 52a/52b data is stale or unavailable. A detailed FAT test procedure for these conditions will be issued ahead of the scheduled FAT date.

COMMERCIAL NOTE

Energize has completed the engineering and hardware deliverables presently achievable prior to final B&V design release and site readiness. Any remaining implementation, FAT, SAT, commissioning, or revised-design support is subject to the applicable original scope and additional written authorization. A structured support arrangement was proposed on June 10, 2026, with a requested commencement date of June 16, 2026 (Option 1: EMS-side support USD 7,500/month · Option 2: Full microgrid scope USD 13,000/month). IMPLEMENTATION LIMITATION

Energize's acceptance in this memorandum is limited to the conceptual division of control responsibility described in Section 2. It does not constitute approval of any final schematic, wiring diagram, terminal schedule, SELogic equation, relay settings file, protection study, field installation, or as-built condition. Those items remain subject to document review, FAT/SAT verification, and the responsible EOR's approval.
